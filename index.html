About me

I'm a Hamilton Scholar and a fourth-year undergraduate student at Southern Methodist University. I'm a passionate learner completing majors in Economics, Mathematics, Statistics, and English. I thematically focus my studies on business forecasting, numerical analysis, and finding interdisciplinary solutions to interdisciplinary problems. I am driven by a passion for innovation and intelligent learning, and I'm constantly looking for ways to expand my horizons.

Previously, I was a summer consultant for SAP, where I spearheaded implementations of company-specific ERP to improve financials, accelerate growth, and uphold compliance. At SMU, upon being rewarded the Hamilton Scholarship, I've worked in the ATLAS Experiment. There, I not only participated in the laboratory, but I also budgeted $1M+ for the acquisition of optoelectronic equipment and applied R to forecast future costs. Although my internship in Summer '20 was canceled due to COVID, I used that time to begin the process of publishing my own book of poems Marginal Negligence in conjunction with the SMU English Department.

In my spare time, I enjoy unraveling the world of modern politics, reading Mohsin Hamid, and savoring the most bizarre cuisines.
